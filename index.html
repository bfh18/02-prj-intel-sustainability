Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel’s journey, where innovation meets environmental leadership for a brighter tomorrow.

1968

Intel Founded

Inception of Intel Corporation by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore.

This milestone laid the groundwork for decades of semiconductor innovation and responsible manufacturing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Introduction of the Intel 4004, the world’s first commercial microprocessor.

It revolutionized computing efficiency and set new standards for energy-aware chip design.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086, introducing the x86 architecture.

This architecture underpins billions of devices, continually optimized for power and performance.

1985

386 Processor

Debut of the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture.

Enabled advanced multitasking and laid a path toward more efficient computing.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel records its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions.

This spurred major investments in renewable energy and sustainable manufacturing.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of the RISE strategy (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling).

Marks a comprehensive plan for climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to net-zero Scope 1 & 2 GHG emissions by 2040.

Underlines Intel’s leadership in decarbonizing global operations.

2023

99% Renewable Power

Achieved 99% renewable electricity use globally.

A key milestone in reducing Intel’s carbon footprint dramatically.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Hosted Intel’s first global Sustainability Summit.

Brought together leaders to drive next-gen sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
